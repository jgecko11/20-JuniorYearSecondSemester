Kent Denver Robotics

  Kent Denver offers one of the most prestigious robotics programs in Colorado. Members can join the program starting in sixth grade, and it is offered to all middle and high school students. We have four high school teams (A, E, W, X) and two middle school teams (N, R). Our teams compete in the VEX robotics competition and have made numerous appearances at the state, national, and international levels.

The Sun Devils

  All of our six teams compete under the identity of 3946 - Sun Devils. Our teams strive not only to build quality machines but also to create an environment that promotes collaboration and learning. Competing as a Sun Devil, members not only learn long-term engineering skills, but also leadership and teamwork. We challenge ourselves to graduate from tinkers to engineers by utilizing an iterative design process multiple times throughout the season. All of our members learn to build, CAD, and code, along with many other useful skills. Experienced members are put into a position of leadership to teach the younger members of their team these skills. Our Mission Statement reads:

"Here at Kent Denver robotics, we believe in striving for excellence by valuing our members over the success of our robot. We seek to build an educated community with a passion for robotics that will enhance the long term work ethic, engineering, teamwork, and motivational skills of every member."

  The program was founded in 2010 by teaching fellow and alumnus Bryan Rich '06. Now an engineer at AECOM, Coach Rich led Kent Denver Robotics to national and international prominence with multiple state titles and VEX World Championship Awards.

  This website was created by members of the club with the hopes of leaving valuable resources for younger members of the club, and also as a tool to reach out to other members of the VEX robotics community. In this website, you will find pages for each high school team, as well as a history and resources page.
