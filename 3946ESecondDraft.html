Main Content

  3946E, Ender, was created in 2015 for the game Nothing But Net. Ender qualifies for both the State and World Championships that year, winning the State Championship and a Build award at Worlds. Since then Ender has never looked back, qualifying for every State and World championship winning a Create award (2017) and a Design award (2018).

“To learn about science, technology, engineering, and math through fun, innovative, and fulfilling team-oriented projects in robotics.”

  The purpose of the 3946E robot is to compete as effectively as possible in the VEX competition while maintaining the high engineering and mathematical standards set by the team. The robot is built and designed by the Kent Denver high schoolers to educate ourselves about the skills required for engineering and robotics.

  3946E are currently competing at the 2021 World Championship, and Junior captain Jack ('22) wishes to pre-qualify for next years ("game name") World Championships in order to keep their perfect worlds streak alive!
